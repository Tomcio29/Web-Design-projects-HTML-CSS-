Introduction

The Grand Canyon

A natural wonder located in Arizona, USA, the Grand Canyon is a steep-sided gorge carved by the Colorado River. It is over 277 miles long, up to 18 miles wide, and over a mile deep. Visitors can hike, raft, or take a helicopter tour to experience the stunning views of the canyon and its colorful layers. It is also a popular spot for stargazing and camping.

The Grand Canyon: Arizona, USA, around 6 million years old, North America, 7,000 km from Poland.
Angkor Wat

A UNESCO World Heritage site in Cambodia, Angkor Wat is a temple complex built in the 12th century. It is the largest religious monument in the world and a masterpiece of Khmer architecture. Visitors can marvel at the intricate carvings, explore the ruins of the ancient Khmer civilization, and witness the stunning sunrise over the temple.

Angkor Wat: Siem Reap, Cambodia, built in the 12th century, Asia, 8,500 km from Poland.
Taj Mahal

A white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India, the Taj Mahal is one of the world's most iconic landmarks. It was built in the 17th century by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Visitors can admire the intricate marble work and the beautiful gardens surrounding the monument.

Taj Mahal: Agra, India, built in the 17th century, Asia, 4,500 km from Poland.
The pyramids of Giza

Located near Cairo, Egypt, the pyramids of Giza are a collection of ancient monuments that include the Great Pyramid, the Sphinx, and other smaller pyramids. Built over 4,500 years ago, they are a testament to the architectural and engineering prowess of the ancient Egyptians. Visitors can explore the pyramids' chambers and learn about the pharaohs who commissioned them.

The pyramids of Giza: Cairo, Egypt, built over 4,500 years ago, Africa, 3,000 km from Poland.
The Amazon rainforest

The world's largest tropical rainforest, the Amazon covers over 2.7 million square miles across nine countries in South America. It is home to an incredible array of plant and animal species, many of which are unique to the region. Visitors can take guided tours to learn about the rainforest's ecology and the indigenous communities that call it home.

The Amazon rainforest: Brazil, existing for millions of years, South America, 9,500 km from Poland.

The Colosseum

An iconic symbol of ancient Rome, the Colosseum is an amphitheater built over 2,000 years ago. It was used for gladiator contests, public spectacles, and other events. Visitors can explore the underground chambers, the arena floor, and learn about the Colosseum's fascinating history.

The Colosseum: Rome, Italy, built over 2,000 years ago, Europe, 1,200 km from Poland.

Ha Long Bay

A UNESCO World Heritage site in Vietnam, Ha Long Bay is a scenic seascape of over 1,600 limestone islands and islets. Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the caves and grottoes, go kayaking, or take a swim in the clear waters. It is a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists.

Ha Long Bay: Northeastern Vietnam, existing for millions of years, Asia, 8,000 km from Poland.

Honourable places to check on your own
  • Machu Picchu: Located in Peru, this ancient Incan city is situated high in the Andes Mountains and is renowned for its impressive architecture and stunning mountain views.
  • Petra: Located in Jordan, Petra is an ancient city carved into the rock face, featuring impressive temples, tombs, and other structures that are thousands of years old.
  • The Great Wall of China: A series of fortifications in northern China, the Great Wall stretches over 13,000 miles and was built over many centuries to protect China from invading forces.
  • The Northern Lights: Also known as Aurora Borealis, this natural phenomenon is a breathtaking display of colorful lights that dance across the night sky in high-latitude regions such as Norway, Sweden, and Iceland.
  • The Serengeti: A vast ecosystem spanning Tanzania and Kenya, the Serengeti is known for its incredible wildlife, including lions, elephants, zebras, and wildebeests, and is home to one of the world's most spectacular natural wonders, the Great Migration.
