Zlatan Ibrahimović

Man renowned for his acrobatic strikes, volleys and powerful long-range shots.

Zlatan Ibrahimović is a Swedish professional footballer who plays as a forward for AC Milan. He is currently the third-most decorated active footballer in the world, having won 31 trophies in his career. He has scored over 520 senior career goals for club and country.

Here's a time line of Zlatan Ibrahimović's life:

  • 1981- Born in Malmö, Sweden, on 3 October. He grew up poor, one of five children living in a dangerous housing project in the center of Malmo, Sweden's third-largest city. He spoke with a lisp, was self-conscious about his big nose and admits he went to school mainly for the free lunches.
  • 1996- First contract with Malmö FF
  • 2001- First international match for Sweden against Faroe Islands.Ibrahimović was eligible to represent Sweden, Bosnia and Herzegovina or Croatia at international level; he chose Sweden
  • 2001- Moving to Ajax FC for 80 million Swedish kronor (€8.7 million)
  • 2004- Moving to Juwentus. November 2005, he was awarded the Guldbollen, a prize awarded to the best Swedish footballer of the year
  • 2006- Moving To Inter Milan. He scored in his Serie A debut. His First Champions League appearance For Inter
  • 2008- Serie A Footballer of the Year.The Goal of The Year In Serie A:during the match against Bologna at home which Inter won 2–1, Ibrahimović scored a sensational goal, stunning Francesco Antonioli with an irresistible backheeled shot from Adriano's left-wing cross
  • 2009- Moving to FC Barcelona. Ibrahimović passed his medical and was presented to a crowd of over 60,000 at Camp Nou. He signed a five-year contract.On 7 November, however, he suffered a thigh injury that kept him out for three weeks
  • 2011- Moving to A.C. Milan. All agreements between Milan and Barcelona were confirmed on 18 June. Serie A Top Scorer
  • 2012- Moving To PSG. He scored a total of 156 goals in 180 games and won 12 trophies, including winning Ligue 1 all four seasons he was there. He is the all time top goalscorer in PSG
  • 2014- Winning the 2013 FIFA Puskás Award: a 35-yard overhead kick with his back to goal, won him praise from players and pundits, with the BBC describing it as a goal that "combined unfathomable imagination and expert technique"
  • 2016- Moving to manchester United. On 20 April 2017, Ibrahimović sustained serious ligament damage in his right knee in a Europa League quarter-final against Anderlecht at Old Trafford
  • 2018- On 23 March 2018, Ibrahimović signed for Major League Soccer (MLS) club LA Galaxy. Scored 500th goal in professional career
  • 2019- Returning to A.C. milan. Zlatan is currently 41 years old and plays in Milan since today
